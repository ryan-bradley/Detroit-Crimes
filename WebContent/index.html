Cluster Maps

Presented below is a map that illustrates the City of Detroit's crime activity from January 2015 until July 2016. Each of the Scout Car Area's (SCA) that are patrolled by the Detroit Police have been clustered/grouped into 5 different clusters. These clusters were calculated and determined using the KMeans Clustering algorithm. This process has been completed 3 times to reflect similar SCA's in the Detroit area based off months, crime category and two hour time periods.

Frequency Distributions

The increase in crimes in March, April, May and June can be attributed to the increased tourism during the Spring and Summer months and hence more people and opportunities for crimes to occur. We chose to generate this chart in order to find out when the safest travel months are for tourists and travellers.

Aside from the 'Miscellaneous' category the 'Assault' category has the highest frequency. Due to 'Miscellaneous' being such a large portion of reported crimes within the DPD database, we believe that this could lead to the City of Detroit being a much safer area than initially expected. However, this can not be confirmed without further extensive investigation. We chose to generate this chart to explore the prevalence of each category of crime, particularly the more severe crimes like homicide and assault.

As exhibited in the figure above there is a drop in crime rate between 0400 and 0700, while there is a spike at noon to 1300. We chose to generate this chart in order to discover the safest times to do activities and explore the area of Detroit.
